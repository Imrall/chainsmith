    Now, let's dive into the step-by-step process of setting up your own subchain on Theta Metachain...

  • We recommend keeping this page open in a separate window while building your first subchain:

    KeyForge
    To get started, add your desired keystores to the KeyForge. Keystores are critical in ensuring the safety of your digital assets.
    GenesisForge
    Proceed by adding or forging a new subchain genesis through the GenesisForge. This process establishes the starting block of your subchain. Be sure to backup your subchain genesis and config.yaml files by exporting them and securing them safely using the download tools available once your genesis is forged. They contain vital information that you will need to restore your subchain in case of any unforeseen disruptions.
    TokenForge
    Configure your Governance Token by linking an existing contract or creating a new contract and deploying it to the Mainchain by utilizing the embedded Theta Metachain SDK. This token will be used to manage and govern your subchain.
    ChainForge
    Create a new subchain in your local registry and register your new Subchain to the Mainchain. This action establishes the connection between your subchain and the Mainchain, and requires 10K wTHETA in the executor wallet.
    StakeForge
    Stake governance tokens to your Subchain Validator Nodes For a robust subchain, add new validators and ensure they are properly staked.
    NodeForge
    Next, add a Mainnet Walletnode and a Subchain Validator in NodeManager. Walletnodes and their corresponding ETH-RPC servers provide mainchain interop services, while subchain validators are responsible for creating new blocks on your subchain. Nodes can be independently deployed or deployed all-at-once upon subchain configuration completion via the Metachain Configurator. While nodes can be configured to operate on different systems, we recommend installing both on the same system.
